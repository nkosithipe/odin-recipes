Phuthu-pap Recipe

Phuthu-pap is an old popular dish among the South African natives and it is usually enjoyed with sour milk as a relish.

The maize meal is cooked in such a way that the complete product looks crumbly and feels soft in texture during mastication.

I will quickly share with you my family's traditional secret ingredient as well as it's custom feature procedure within the general method to make your Phuthu-pap so tasty you may even be tempted to enjoy it without any relish.

Ingredients

  • 200ml water
  • 4 cups maize meal of your choice
  • 1 Tsp salt
  • 5 grams of margarine (Mama's secret)

Method

  1. Bring the water and salt to boil in a pot.
  2. Reduce the heat to medium and add the maize meal,
  3. Use a fork to stir mostly in cutting motion from center of the pot towards its edges right-around until contents are crumbly.
  4. Cover with a lid and leave simmering for 15 minutes
  5. Stir once more in the same manner as described above at step 2 and add Magarine, sprinkling it all over the top.
  6. Immediately set your heat to Low after adding Magarine and leave simmering again for 5 minutes this time around still with the lid on.
  7. Stir one last time and ensure the melted Margarine covers all crumbs till the meal is golden yellow
  8. Switch off your stove and allow cooling to the degree of your liking.
